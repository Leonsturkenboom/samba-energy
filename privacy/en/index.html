SAMBA PRIVACY

Privacy Policy

SAMBA Energy — Version February 2025

SAMBA Energy (hereinafter: ‘SAMBA’) attaches great importance to the careful handling of your personal data. We process your personal data in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other applicable privacy legislation.

In this Privacy Policy, we explain which personal data we collect, why we do so, how long we retain it, and what rights you have. This policy applies to all services that SAMBA offers through https://samba.energy.

Article 1 – Who are we?

SAMBA Energy is the controller responsible for the processing of your personal data as described in this Privacy Policy.

SAMBA Energy, Ooster Ringdijk 24, 1098TW Amsterdam, The Netherlands.

If you have questions about your personal data or wish to exercise any of your rights, please contact us at privacy@samba.energy.

Article 2 – What personal data do we process?

Depending on the way you contact us or use our services, we process the following categories of personal data:

2.1 Contact form, demo request, and cost-savings form

When you complete one of our online forms — the general contact form, the demo request form, or the cost-savings request form — we process the data you provide. This includes, among other things, your name, (business) email address, telephone number, company name, job title, and the content of your message or request.

We retain this data not only to handle your request, but also to contact you at a later time with relevant information about our services. We do this on the basis of legitimate interest (business follow-up of an initial enquiry) and/or your consent, depending on how you completed the form. See also Article 3.

2.2 Communication via email and telephone

When you contact us directly by email or telephone, we process your name, contact details, and the content of the conversation or correspondence.

2.3 Website usage and cookies

When you visit our website, technical and behavioural data is automatically collected. Depending on your cookie preferences, this may include: your IP address, browser type, device, pages visited, duration of the visit, referral source (e.g. via search engine or social media), and interactions with page elements such as forms and buttons. For more information, please refer to Article 7 (Cookies).

2.4 Business contacts

Data of contact persons at companies or organisations is processed in the same manner as data of other data subjects.

Article 3 – On what legal basis and for what purpose do we process your data?

We process personal data exclusively on the basis of one of the following legal grounds under the GDPR:

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR): you have given explicit consent, for example by ticking an opt-in box on a form or by subscribing to our newsletter.
  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR): the processing is necessary for the performance or preparation of a contract with you.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR): we are legally required to process or retain certain data.
  • Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR): we have a legitimate interest in the processing. Examples include: business follow-up of submitted enquiries, fraud prevention, improvement of our services, and security.

3.1 Commercial follow-up of form submissions

Data submitted through the contact form, the demo request form, or the cost-savings form is retained by us for commercial follow-up. This means that we may contact you at a later time with information about our services that relates to your earlier enquiry.

For short-term follow-up of a submitted enquiry (within six months), we rely on legitimate interest. If we wish to contact you for a longer period or for broader marketing purposes, we will request your explicit consent via an opt-in checkbox on the form. You may withdraw your consent at any time via privacy@samba.energy.

Article 4 – How long do we retain your data?

We do not retain your personal data for longer than is strictly necessary for the purposes for which it was collected. We apply the following retention periods as a guideline:

  • Contact details and submitted forms (contact form, demo request, cost savings): for the duration of the relationship and up to a maximum of two (2) years after the last contact, or for as long as your consent for commercial follow-up remains in effect.
  • Communication by email or telephone: for the duration of the relationship and up to a maximum of two (2) years thereafter.
  • Business contacts: up to seven (7) years after the end of the business relationship, in accordance with fiscal retention obligations.
  • Analytical website data (anonymised): a maximum of twelve (12) months after your last visit.
  • Newsletter preferences: for as long as you are subscribed, plus five (5) years after unsubscribing.
  • Data relating to legal proceedings: for the duration of the proceedings and up to five (5) years thereafter.

Fully anonymised and aggregated data may be retained for analytical purposes for a longer period.

Article 5 – Sharing with third parties

SAMBA does not share your personal data with third parties, unless:

  • This is necessary for the provision of our services (for example CRM software, email marketing tools, analytical platforms, or hosting providers), with whom we have concluded a data processing agreement;
  • We are legally obliged to do so or a competent authority requests it;
  • This takes place in the context of a merger, acquisition, or corporate reorganisation, in which case we will request your consent where required by law.

We never sell your personal data to third parties and do not share it for the commercial purposes of third parties without your explicit consent.

Article 6 – Transfers outside the EEA

We primarily process your personal data within the European Economic Area (EEA). If it is necessary to transfer data outside the EEA, we ensure that this only takes place to countries with an adequate level of protection as determined by the European Commission, or on the basis of appropriate safeguards such as EU Standard Contractual Clauses.

Article 7 – Cookies

SAMBA uses cookies on its website. Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device via the browser. We distinguish the following types of cookies:

7.1 Functional cookies (always active)

These cookies are necessary for the technical functioning of the website, such as remembering your session and your cookie preferences. They cannot be disabled.

7.2 Analytical cookies

Analytical cookies allow us to measure how visitors use the website: which pages are visited, how long someone stays on a page, through which channel the visitor arrived (search engine, social media, direct link), and which devices and browsers are used. The collected data is anonymised where possible. These cookies are used exclusively to improve the website and our services.

7.3 Marketing and tracking cookies

With your consent, we may place marketing and tracking cookies. These cookies enable us to link your website visit to advertising platforms (such as Google Ads, Meta Pixel, or LinkedIn Insight Tag) in order to:

  • show targeted advertisements to visitors who have previously visited our website (retargeting);
  • measure the effectiveness of marketing campaigns;
  • build so-called ‘lookalike audiences’ based on anonymised visitor profiles.

Marketing and tracking cookies are only placed after your explicit consent via the cookie banner. You may withdraw your consent at any time via the cookie settings on the website.

7.4 Session cookies

Temporary cookies that are automatically deleted when you close your browser.

7.5 Cookie management

You may change your cookie preferences at any time via the cookie settings on our website or through your browser settings. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of the website. More information about cookies can be found on the website of the Dutch Consumers’ Association (www.consumentenbond.nl).

Article 8 – Security of your personal data

We take the protection of your personal data seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to safeguard your data against unauthorised access, loss, misuse, or alteration. These measures include, among others:

  • Encrypted data transfer via secure connections (HTTPS/TLS);
  • Access restriction: only employees who require the data for their duties have access;
  • Confidentiality obligations for employees with access to personal data;
  • Regular updates of our systems with the latest security patches.

If you suspect that your data is insufficiently secured or there are indications of a data breach, please contact us immediately at privacy@samba.energy.

Article 9 – Data breach notification

In the event of a data breach, we immediately assess the nature and severity of the incident. If there is a high risk to your rights and freedoms, we report this to the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens) and, where required, inform you accordingly. We will specify the nature of the breach, the potential consequences, and the measures we are taking to limit the damage.

Article 10 – Your rights as a data subject

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights with respect to your personal data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR): you may request which personal data we process about you.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR): you may have inaccurate or incomplete data corrected.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR): you may request that your data be deleted (‘right to be forgotten’).
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR): you may have the processing of your data (temporarily) restricted.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR): you may request your data in a machine-readable format or have it transferred to a third party.
  • Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR): you may object to the processing of your data, in particular for direct marketing purposes. We will then cease the processing, unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds.
  • Right to withdraw consent: where the processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise your rights, please send an email to privacy@samba.energy. We will respond to your request within four weeks. We reserve the right to refuse a request if there is a compelling legal basis for doing so.

Article 11 – Complaints

If you have a complaint about the way we process your personal data, please first contact us at privacy@samba.energy. We are happy to assist you. In addition, you always have the right to file a complaint with the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens) via www.autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl.

Article 12 – Links to external websites

The SAMBA website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the content or privacy policy of these external websites. We recommend that you review the privacy policy of the websites concerned.

Article 13 – Changes to this Privacy Policy

SAMBA reserves the right to amend this Privacy Policy. Changes will be published on https://samba.energy. We recommend that you consult this page regularly. Significant changes that affect your rights will be actively communicated via the website or by email.
